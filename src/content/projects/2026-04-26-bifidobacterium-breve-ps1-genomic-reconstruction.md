## Overview

This project reconstructs and functionally profiles the genome of *Bifidobacterium breve* PS1, a breastmilk-derived strain. The work combines long-read genome assembly, annotation, comparative genomics, and carbohydrate utilization analysis.

The [CAZyme](https://www.cazy.org/) and [GlycoBif](https://github.com/Arzamasov/glycobif) analyses reveal how the strain is equipped to process host-associated glycans and prebiotic substrates.

## CAZyme and GlycoBif Findings

CAZyme profiling showed that *B. breve* PS1 carries a carbohydrate-active enzyme dominated by glycoside hydrolases (61 genes), followed by glycosyl transferases (33 genes) and carbohydrate esterases (5 genes).

GlycoBif analysis predicted that *B. breve* PS1 can utilize key infant-associated substrates including lacto-N-tetraose (LNT), lacto-N-neotetraose (LNnT), lacto-N-biose (LNB), and N-acetylneuraminic acid (Neu5Ac). The same analysis also supported utilization of galactooligosaccharides (GOS) and short-chain fructooligosaccharides (scFOS).

These results suggest that *B. breve* PS1 is well adapted to early-life gut ecology, can participate in HMO-linked cross-feeding networks, and may remain metabolically competitive during the transition from breastfeeding to more complex dietary substrates.

## Immunomodulatory Potential

The immunostimulatory potential of *B. breve* PS1 was characterized through in silico CpG motif profiling and island mapping. Both type 1 (palindromic) and type 2 hexamers were quantified and yielding a combined total of 20,199 CpG motifs. This density of immunostimulatory motifs supports the capacity of *B. breve* PS1 to engage innate immune pathways.

Beyond CpG-based immune engagement, the genome encodes Serpin, a serine protease inhibitor involved in modulating host inflammatory proteases. The strain also carries complete gene clusters for exopolysaccharide (EPS) production, including a full *cps4* locus encoding the priming glycosyltransferase Cps4H, O-antigen polymerase, and associated EpsF/EpsH glycosyltransferases. EPS production is associated with immunomodulatory interactions at the mucosal surface and enhanced colonization persistence.
